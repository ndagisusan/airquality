what we do

We analyze the air quality of an area and perform an analysis on the sensor data obtained.This is achievable due to the IoT integrated system designed for this purpose that measures the air quality using locally available sensors.

Graphs

The pollutant gas levels are displayed in real time in a graphical format. The data is compared against the temperature and humidity of the area.

CHARTS

The pollutant gas portions are compared on charts for better visual representation of the gas levels. This gives a general overview of the air quality of the area.

ANALYSIS

An analysis is done on the pollutant gas levels to determine if they are within the allowable limits as per the Air Quality Regulations by NEMA.

The pollutant gases measured by our system are:

1. Particulate Matter: PM 2.5 and PM 10
Particulate Matter (PM) is one of the deadliest air pollutants that varies in size from 0.3-10um. It is responsible for most of the respiratory infections that have lead to about 7 million premature deaths worldwide so far.
‍
2. Carbon II oxide (Monoxide) - CO
CO is a colourless, odourless gas that is formed when there is not enough oxygen when burning carbon based fuels. It is also a very deadly gas.
‍
3. Carbon IV oxide (Di-oxide) - CO2
CO2 is a naturally occurring gas in the atmosphere. However, its levels have been increasing making it one of the major causes of the increasing greenhouse effect that leads to increased rate of global warming.

about air quality kenya

Air Quality Kenya aims on providing the necessary data on the air quality of different areas in Kenya. It aims on mitigating air pollution and consequently reducing the causes and effects of global warming. It provides a solution for SDG 13 which focuses on Climate Action.
